Alan Turing

“The father of modern computing”

Timeline

  • 1912 - Born in London, England
  • 1926 - Enrolls at Sherborne boarding school in Dorset
  • 1931 - Begins studying mathematics at King's College, Cambridge
  • 1936 - Publishes his paper "On Computable Numbers" introducing the concept of a Turing machine
  • 1938 - Obtains his PhD in mathematics from Princeton University
  • 1939 - Joins the Government Code and Cypher School at Bletchley Park to work on breaking the Enigma code
  • 1940 - Specifies the electromechanical bombe machine to help break Enigma more effectively
  • 1941 - Sends the "Action This Day" memo to Winston Churchill stressing the importance of more resources for Bletchley Park
  • 1942 - Travels to the US to share his expertise on cryptography and consult on speech encryption devices
  • 1945 - Begins work designing the ACE (Automatic Computing Engine) at the National Physical Laboratory
  • 1948 - Appointed reader in mathematics at Victoria University of Manchester
  • 1950 - Publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" introducing the Turing test for machine intelligence
  • 1951 - Publishes "The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis" on mathematical biology and pattern formation
  • 1952 - Convicted of gross indecency for his homosexuality and agrees to chemical castration
  • 1954 - Dies from cyanide poisoning just before his 42nd birthday, ruled a suicide
  • 2009 - British Prime Minister Gordon Brown makes an official apology for Turing's prosecution and treatment

“It is the people who no one imagined anything of who do the things no one can imagine.”

- Alan Turing
